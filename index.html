The Team

Jacqueline Blackwell, Ph.D., P.Geo.

Principal Consulting Geologist and President

Jacqueline has a Ph.D. in volcanology and economic geology from the University of Tasmania (CODES), a B.Sc. in Earth and Ocean Science from the University of Victoria, and 14 years in the exploration industry. She is a registered Professional Geologist with the Engineers and Geoscientists British Columbia (EGBC) and the Professional Geoscientists Ontario (PGO).
Jacqueline is a specialist in recognition and classification of volcanic and hydrothermal lithofacies in altered and mineralized terranes with exploration experience in orogenic gold, porphyry copper-gold, and epithermal gold deposits. She has contributed to various national and international advanced exploration and mining projects in Canada, Papua New Guinea, Fiji, Philippines, Africa, South America, and Scandinavia.
For the past five years, Jacqueline has been consulting to Gold Candle Ltd., where she leads a team that has compiled and analyzed 58 years of historical production records and conducted the first modern day exploration program on the iconic Kerr-Addison Mine project in the Abitibi Greenstone Belt, northern Ontario.

Tim Stubley, B.Sc.(Hons)

Principal Consulting Geologist and Vice President

Tim got his start in the exploration industry 18 years ago as a field-hand on projects in northern Quebec and central British Columbia, Canada, and in 2010 completed a B.Sc. with Honours in Geology from the University of Tasmania. He is currently undertaking a modular-based Masters in Mineral Exploration at Laurentian University.
Tim specializes in exploration project management including mapping and drilling programs in brownfields and remote helicopter-supported field settings. His geologic specialty is sedimentary environments and he continues to develop his skills in structurally complex settings. Throughout his career he has managed exploration programs in British Columbia, Yukon, and Ontario, Canada, and has contributed to projects in Utah, U.S.A., the Pilbara, western Tasmania, and Cadia districts in Australia, and in northern Finland.
